Akram Almohalwas

My name is Akram Almohalwas, I've been a lecturer at the statistics department at UCLA since 2010. I got promoted to a continuing lecturer last year. I had my PhD in mathematics with statistics concentration from Central Michigan University. I initially worked on data mining techniques applied on gene expression data while working on my PhD dissertation. I always loved sports and following different kind of sports, especially Tennis and Basketball. I thought that using my statistical knowledge and my love for the sport I've decided to help undergraduate student to conduct little research using NBA data. Students usually are asked to read some literature regarding data mining and NBA data related research. I usually provide the research ideas to students and guide them through the research process. I get the pleasure of witnessing the AHA moments when students statistics knowledge is put to use in real world applications. I'm happy to give my students the research experience regardless how little it can be.
